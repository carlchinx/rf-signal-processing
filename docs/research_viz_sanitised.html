Transmission & Group Delay A — Dual axis · left: insertion loss (dB) · right: group delay (ns)
Group delay is the derivative of phase with respect to frequency — it measures how long each frequency component takes to traverse the filter. A flat group delay preserves waveform shape; the pronounced peak at the band edges (~4–7 ns) means signals near the cut-off are delayed relative to mid-band, causing dispersion in wideband signals.
Time-Domain Impulse Response derived · IFFT B — Computed via inverse FFT of S21
If you fired a perfect 1-nanosecond impulse into this filter, this is what would emerge. The ~3 ns initial delay is the propagation time through the structure. The 12–14 ns ringing tail is the filter's stored energy — a direct consequence of its steep skirts. Sharper cut-off in frequency always means longer ringing in time (time–bandwidth uncertainty).
Power Partition derived · passivity C — Transmitted + Reflected + Dissipated = 1
Every watt into the filter is accounted for: transmitted (useful), reflected (bounced back to source), or dissipated (heat in the resonators). In the stopband, almost all power is reflected — the filter is reactive, not absorptive. The ~13% dissipation floor in the passband represents conductor and dielectric losses.
Manufacturing Spread — Deviation from Ensemble Mean derived · Δ from μ D — Each unit's S21 minus the mean of all 4 units · passband 3.2–5.0 GHz
Removing the shared response reveals what distinguishes each unit from the others — the manufacturing fingerprint. Unit Unit 1 sits above mean at the low-frequency band edge; Unit 4 is below. The deviation grows sharply at both band edges (±10 dB), where small geometric tolerances in the resonator coupling produce large transmission changes. The flat region (~3.7–4.4 GHz) is the robust core: units agree to within ±0.3 dB here regardless of unit-to-unit variation.
S11 Impedance Locus — Smith Chart E — Input reflection coefficient on normalised Γ-plane · 0.057–10 GHz
The input impedance traces a complex trajectory as frequency sweeps. In the stopband the locus hugs the outer rim (|Γ|≈1: near-total reflection). In the passband it spirals toward the chart centre (matched impedance, low reflection). The four units' loci overlay tightly — good impedance consistency across the production batch.
S21 Phase Constellation — Polar F — Complex S21 · magnitude encoded as radius · frequency sweeps as angle
Each point is the complex transmission coefficient at one frequency. The spiralling pattern arises from the continuously rotating phase while amplitude rises through the passband. The dense outer ring is the passband; the tight cluster near the origin is the stopband (|S21| ≈ 0).
